MBBS Abroad Admission Process

While each country has its own set of rules and eligibility criteria for international students wanting to pursue MBBS abroad, we'll discuss general eligibility guidelines for Indian students.

Academic Requirements

  • The student must have completed 10+2 from a recognized board.
  • Minimum aggregate of 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology with English as a compulsory subject.
  • Qualification in the National Eligibility-cum-Entrance Test (NEET) is mandatory.
  • Students must provide an Eligibility Certificate at the time of admission.

Medical Requirements

Students need a medical certificate confirming they are free from contagious diseases.

Application Process

Once eligibility criteria are met, students proceed with:

  • Selecting a preferred university
  • Filling out the online international student application form
  • Submitting required documents including academic transcripts, passport, language proficiency certificates, proof of funds, and more.

Exams Required

Indian students must pass NEET. Depending on university requirements, English proficiency tests like TOEFL, IELTS, PTE, or OET may also be necessary.

Upon submission, applicants receive an admission letter and proceed with obtaining a Student Visa.
